# Institutional Layout Benchmarks

## Purpose

This reference records reusable information-design patterns observed in official publications from major financial institutions. It does not reproduce proprietary artwork, house styles, charts, or page designs. Apply the patterns to the SPD Bank briefing in an original layout.

## Official Benchmarks

| Institution | Official publication | Reusable pattern |
|---|---|---|
| J.P. Morgan Asset Management | [Guide to the Markets](https://am.jpmorgan.com/us/en/asset-management/adv/insights/market-insights/guide-to-the-markets/) | Clear masthead, dated edition, front-loaded highlights, chart-led thematic pages, and visible source notes |
| UBS | [House View](https://www.ubs.com/global/en/wealthmanagement/insights/house-view.html) | Monthly-letter opening, messages in focus, implementation view, and a deliberate path from thesis to action |
| Goldman Sachs Research | [Top of Mind](https://www.goldmansachs.com/pdfs/insights/pages/gs-research/top-of-mind-stagflation-risk/report.pdf) | Strong issue framing, executive overview before detailed evidence, and disciplined separation of views and supporting material |
| Standard Chartered | [Global Market Outlook in brief](https://www.sc.com/en/uploads/sites/66/content/docs/wm-global-market-outlook-strategies-for-a-world-of-falling-rates-01-october-2024.pdf) | Top preferences, compact allocation tables, short rationale blocks, and clear distinction between core and opportunistic ideas |
| HSBC Global Private Banking | [Investment Outlook](https://www.privatebanking.hsbc.com/wih/investments-Insights/House-views/2026-investment-outlook-where-capital-meets-the-future/) | A small number of high-conviction themes, dense but readable evidence blocks, and direct source lines for charts and data |
| Bank of England | [Monetary Policy Report](https://www.bankofengland.co.uk/monetary-policy-report/2026/april-2026) | Prominent report status/date, summary before analytical chapters, restrained public-institution styling, and formal end matter |
| BlackRock Investment Institute | [Weekly commentary](https://www.blackrock.com/us/individual/insights/blackrock-investment-institute/weekly-commentary) | Repeatable sequence of market take, backdrop, week ahead, bottom line, and tactical-view tables |
| Deutsche Bank Private Bank | [CIO View](https://wealth.db.com/dam/deutschewealth/landing-pages/events/2026/experts-in-house-perspectives-cio-view/eiheng-clientready.pdf) | Concise theme pages, key messages adjacent to evidence, consistent section hierarchy, and disclosures at the end |

## Pipeline F Design Decisions

Translate those patterns into this briefing as follows:

1. Use a branded masthead with report period and `内部使用` status visible on the first page and in the running header.
2. Open with three to five conclusions and a three-to-six-row opportunity priority table. A senior reader should understand the commercial agenda before reaching the first story.
3. Use the fixed story sequence `摘要` → `关键影响` → `商机切入点` → `后续关注` → image/source. This is the briefing's equivalent of view → implication → action → trigger.
4. Keep one development per story. Do not create decorative section cards or marketing-style hero pages.
5. Prefer one decision-useful official chart or corporate image for a major theme. Every embedded visual needs a direct caption and owner/source line; omit weak or rights-unclear imagery.
6. Use tables for prioritisation, Companies House changes, and dated watch items. Keep prose evidence outside tables when it would make cells unreadable.
7. Keep the normal edition short enough to scan without a contents page. Add a contents page only if a future edition consistently exceeds 10 pages.
8. Put method, coverage limitations, source discipline, and the full disclaimer in the final section.
9. Use restrained SPD Bank blue, red accents only for priority or classification, compact typography, fixed Letter-page geometry, and generous white space around section headings.
10. Preserve source traceability even when the document is printed: source names and URLs must remain in the DOCX.

## Anti-Patterns

- Do not imitate a benchmark institution's proprietary cover, chart design, wording, or visual identity.
- Do not lead with a decorative cover that delays the executive conclusions.
- Do not use stock photography merely to make the report look illustrated.
- Do not crowd every page with charts; visual density must follow analytical value.
- Do not bury report status, coverage limitations, or disclaimer language.
