# Selection and Opportunity Rubric

## Scope

Keep developments within the requested date range that materially affect one or more of:

- the UK operating, financing, investment, trade, regulatory, or demand environment;
- a Chinese enterprise expanding, investing, producing, selling, acquiring, or restructuring in Europe;
- a cross-border M&A transaction involving a Chinese or Hong Kong principal and a UK/European target, seller, asset, or regulator;
- a Chinese enterprise's greenfield, brownfield, R&D, logistics, retail, energy, infrastructure, data-centre, or financial-services footprint in the UK or Europe;
- an important UK entity whose Chinese nexus and Companies House change are adequately supported.

Routine product launches, generic speeches, recycled commentary, immaterial appointments, unsupported rumours, and ordinary filings with no identifiable commercial relevance should drop.

## Source Rules

Prefer evidence in this order:

1. Companies House, regulators, courts, central banks, statistics agencies, government departments, stock-exchange filings, and company investor-relations material.
2. Established international, national, regional, or financial media with accessible factual body text.
3. Reputable specialist publications, law firms, accounting firms, research institutions, and industry bodies when they directly support the claim.

Search snippets are discovery aids, not evidence. Open every source used. Replace inaccessible or paywalled leads with a readable authoritative source covering the same event. A company press release can establish the company's own action but should not be used alone for disputed impact claims.

## Date Rules

- The report covers events published, filed, announced, or materially advanced between `date_from` and `date_to`, inclusive.
- An older background source may support context but cannot be the sole basis for a current development.
- For a continuing M&A process, keep only when the period contains a new bid, agreement, filing, vote, clearance, financing, completion, withdrawal, or other concrete stage change.

## Chinese Nexus

Accept as `confirmed` when supported by one or more of:

- a Chinese or Hong Kong parent company's official disclosure;
- a corporate ownership chain supported by official registry or filing records;
- a Companies House PSC or corporate-controller record that identifies the relevant parent or controlled entity;
- multiple authoritative sources that explicitly identify the Chinese parent relationship.

Accept as `probable` only when a strong, explained evidence chain exists but one ownership link remains indirect. Mark the uncertainty prominently.

Treat as `unverified` when based only on:

- Chinese-sounding personal or company names;
- director nationality or country of residence;
- a registered address shared with Chinese-linked businesses;
- branding similarity without ownership evidence;
- a single unsupported directory or aggregator.

Unverified entities must not be described as Chinese-owned or Chinese-controlled.

## Transaction Stage

Use exactly one stage where applicable:

- `rumour`
- `exploring`
- `non-binding`
- `announced`
- `recommended-offer`
- `signed`
- `regulatory-review`
- `shareholder-approval`
- `financing`
- `completed`
- `withdrawn`
- `blocked`
- `not-applicable`

Drop unsupported rumours. Do not write an announced or signed deal as completed.

## Priority

Assign one:

- `high`: near-term, named, actionable development with a plausible financing, transaction-banking, advisory, risk-management, or relationship opportunity.
- `medium`: strategically relevant development with identifiable affected companies or sectors but a less immediate action window.
- `monitor`: credible signal worth tracking, but evidence of near-term commercial activity is still limited.

Priority is contextual. Do not use a fixed deal-value threshold.

## Banking Opportunity Angles

Use only angles supported by the event:

- acquisition finance, bridge finance, syndicated lending, refinancing;
- project finance, capex finance, asset finance, real-estate finance;
- working capital, supply-chain finance, trade finance, guarantees;
- cash management, liquidity, collections, payments, merchant acquiring;
- FX, rates, commodities, hedging, treasury advisory;
- RMB services, cross-border settlement, capital-account support;
- debt capital markets, equity capital markets, private placements;
- M&A, valuation, structuring, regulatory, or post-merger integration support;
- deposits, payroll, local banking setup, and multi-entity account structures.

Never assert that a company needs, requested, or will buy a product unless a source says so. Write `潜在需求`, `可能切入点`, or `建议核实`.

## Deduplication

Merge reports about the same actors, transaction, action, and stage. Select the most direct and complete primary source; retain corroborating URLs. Keep separate later-stage developments when they contain a new material action.

## Images

Apply `image-policy.md`. Image availability never outweighs evidence quality or commercial importance.
