# Reputation Severity Rules

Loaded only by the Verifier.

- `high`: likely to cause major and sustained harm to trust in the company or a senior executive, materially affect important stakeholders, or demand immediate senior attention.
- `medium`: a concrete adverse development or visible controversy with meaningful but contained reputational consequences.
- `low`: a limited complaint, criticism, allegation, or negative discussion that is relevant but currently narrow in reach or consequence.

Use contextual judgement. There are no numerical thresholds, source tiers, risk categories, credibility scores, or automatic source-based upgrades or downgrades.

Positive, neutral, unrelated, promotional, or unverifiable content is not a finding.

Media allegations must remain allegations unless the source establishes the underlying fact. Social-media content must always be described as a post, complaint, allegation, or discussion, not as confirmed fact.
